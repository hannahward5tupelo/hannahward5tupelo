About Victor

Twenty-five years ago this past December, I took a job in Tupelo to tide me over for a few months between graduating from law school and passing the bar exam. My plan throughout law school was to return from whence I came, Miami, Florida. "Mann Tracht, Un Gott Lacht." "Man plans, and God laughs."


Fast forward to today and this June marks twenty-five years since I moved to Tupelo and began calling it my home. In that time, I've raised three boys to adulthood, all graduates of Tupelo High School, built a small law practice, on a wing and a prayer, and forged friendships with hosts of wonderful people.


As a first-generation son of a Cuban-exile father and Puerto Rican mother, migrants to this great Nation, I've been privileged to live the American Dream with a deep sense of service, gratitude, and humility. Throughout my twenty-five year legal career, I've done my level best to guide people through some of the most difficult and complex moments in their lives. Today, in putting forth this candidacy for the office of Mayor of the City of Tupelo, I hope to take the personal and professional lessons I've learned over my lifetime and apply them to the opportunities and challenges we face as a community, with the same commitment to service, gratitude, and humility.



Issues & Plans

We are blessed to live and work in a wonderful community filled with promise. But make no mistake about it; what we enjoy in Tupelo today came about as a direct result of forward thinking administrations, city councils, city employees, organizations, and residents. Their combined visions for a better and more prosperous Tupelo provided the framework and impetus for the progress we enjoy today. To all who came before and contributed to making Tupelo the finest city in the State of Mississippi, I say, "Thank you!"


Much work remains to be done and greater opportunities lay ahead just waiting to be discovered. How best to achieve the progress we all desire will be the central issue of this campaign and I welcome the opportunity to meet with all of you to learn about your ideas for how we can improve as a City. To jumpstart our conversation, I want to highlight several areas of importance to me:


Victor & Lisa.

Transparency & Accountability

This campaign believes the business of government must be open and transparent, and fully accountable to the People for its successes and failures.

Prudent Financial Management

This campaign believes in continuing the long tradition of prudent and responsible fiscal management by prior administrations and city councils.

Equal Opportunity

This campaign disavows any and all forms of discrimination and bias in employment, in government contracts, and in the provision of all public services.

Education

This campaign believes the ultimate path forward for personal development and progress rests with our education system. We are justifiably proud of our school system. We can do even better. This campaign commits itself to continuing Tupelo's beneficial partnership with the Tupelo Public School District, while actively encouraging a host of stakeholders (community colleges, universities, businesses, and civic organizations and leaders) to work with us to continue to improve our schools, and outcomes and opportunities for our children.

City Employees

City employees perform daily the often difficult jobs which keep our City safe and functioning smoothly. This campaign commits itself to ensuring that all employees of the City of Tupelo, from the police, to fire and rescue, public works, administration, and every other department are provided the best training, pay, and opportunities for advancement and promotion possible.

Infrastructure

This campaign commits itself to not only maintaining, but improving and growing our infrastructure to meet the growing needs of our vibrant community.

Affordable Housing

This campaign commits itself to working with stakeholders in the public and private sectors to develop affordable housing across a spectrum of price ranges to meet all needs. The people who live and work in Tupelo must be able to find affordable housing to achieve the American dream of home ownership.
